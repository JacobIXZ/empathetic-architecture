# Design & Research Notes
Research has shown that cooperative behavior can emerge in simple agent systems under repeated interaction and memory.
- Axelrod (1984) demonstrated that strategies like "Tit-for-Tat" promote stable cooperation in the Iterated Prisoner's Dilemma (direct reciprocity).
- Nowak & Sigmund (1998) showed that indirect reciprocity and reputation systems support cooperation in social groups (indirect reciprocity).
- Multi-agent reinforcement learning studies have also demonstrated emergent cooperation under shared incentives and repeated interaction.

These works primarily focus on strategic cooperation rather than the emergence of empathy-like behavior.

## 1. Motivation
This project investigates whether helping behavior can emerge under constraints in a way that resembles early forms of empathy.
